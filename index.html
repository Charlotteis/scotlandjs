JavaScript keeps growing and growing. It is the language most repos on GitHub are written in!

Scotland JS aims to bring together 30+ local and international speakers and to enable attendees to share their experiences and learn we are introducing a new Discussion Track.

Tickets

Tickets will go onsale early in February.

By purchasing your ticket early (mid-Feb or before) you, the attendee, get to vote for the sessions submitted via the CFP that you wish to see as part of ScotlandJS 2014!

Call For Proposals

The Call For Proposals is now open! Submit your talk proposal here.

You have until 23:59 GMT on Wednesday, February 12th to submit your proposal(s), thereafter a blind vote will be made by ticket holders.

To help speakers with any questions about the event we are running two online chat sessions on

  1. Friday 31st January at 8pm (GMT)
  2. Monday 3rd February at 8pm (GMT)
Details will be announced on twitter 5 minutes before the sessions begin.

Here at ScotlandJS we have one criteria for your talk submission(s); that you submit talks on subjects that you are passionate about. Simple as that.

We would love presentations that are 20 minutes long and of interest to JavaScript developers.

This year we are trying something new with the conference format to encourage more discussion in the community. To achieve this, after each talk a discussion will start in Track 2 based on the subject of that talk. It would be amazing if speakers were available in this session to help explain, clarify and drive a discussion to forward the state-of-the-art, however this is optional.
